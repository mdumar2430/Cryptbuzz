MILLER-RABIN PRIMALITY TEST



Miller-Rabin Primality test:


   The Miller–Rabin test is the most widely used probabilistic primality test. For odd composite n > 1 over 75% of numbers from to 2 to n − 1 are witnesses in the Miller–Rabin test for n. We will describe the test, prove the 75% lower bound (an improvement on the Solovay–Strassen test), and in an appendix use the main idea in the test to show factoring n into primes and computing ϕ(n) are similar computational tasks.
